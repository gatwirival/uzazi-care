# The problem

Women's health support is often:

- **Fragmented** across menstrual, pregnancy, and postpartum stages
- **Reactive instead of preventive**, especially around symptom escalation
- **Hard to access** outside clinic hours
- **Underserved in digital health**, particularly for locally relevant workflows
- **Burdened by documentation gaps** for providers managing multiple patients

## Result
Users delay care, providers spend time on repetitive guidance, and hospitals miss an opportunity to deliver continuous support beyond the facility.

---

# Why now

Three trends make this the right moment:

1. **AI assistants can now deliver structured, stage-specific support** at scale
2. **Women increasingly seek mobile-first health guidance** before and between visits
3. **Hospitals and clinics need affordable digital tools** with payment models that fit local markets

Uzazi Care combines these trends into a focused women's health platform instead of a generic chatbot.

---

# Our solution

Uzazi Care is a women's health companion that supports users through:

- **Menstrual health**: cycle prediction, symptom tracking, guidance
- **Pregnancy**: weekly support, warning signs, hospital prep
- **Postpartum recovery**: recovery monitoring, breastfeeding, mental health prompts
- **AI health chat**: free-text questions plus guided starter prompts
- **Emergency escalation**: detects high-risk symptoms and urges urgent care
- **Education library**: trusted, structured learning content

For providers and hospitals, the platform extends into:

- patient management
- file upload and analysis workflows
- AI-assisted report generation
- role-based access and subscription billing

# How it works

## For women
1. Select life stage
2. Ask a question or choose a suggested prompt
3. Receive stage-specific AI guidance
4. Get immediate escalation when symptoms suggest danger
5. Continue learning through education modules

## For providers
1. Add patients and upload records
2. Use AI chat with patient context
3. Generate reports and risk-oriented summaries
4. Manage care within a hospital-linked subscription system

This creates a bridge between **self-guided education** and **clinical continuity**.
